Basic Concepts

The stratagy of chess is complex and it requires many years of study and hard work to master. However you can learn a few concepts that can help you improve your games. Learning the material count, piece activity, pawn structure, space, and king safety will help you win more games. If you want to know more about Basic Concepts click here: Basic concepts at chess.com.

Openings

Knowing openings can help you move your first pieces with confidence. The Sicilian Defense, French Defense, Ruy Lopez Opening, and the Caro-Kann Defense are the four most popular openings. If you want to know more about openings click here: Chess openings at chess.com

Sicilian Defense

Black's c5 posisition is the most popular response to White's e4 position. This defense is a reputable and sound opening. It is one of the oldest known openings and it's popularity sky rocketed in the second half of the 20th century.

Close

French Defense

One of the most oldest openings in chess. The name comes from an 1834 game between the London Chess Club and the Paris Chess Club. It is a repsonse to white's e4 by moving to e6.

Close

Ruy Lopez Opening

This opening is also known as the Spanish Opening or Spanish Game. It is one of most analyzed openings in chess history. Most adopt this opening as apart of their repertoire. This opening starts after the moves e4 e5(pawn moves to e5 in response to e4) , Nf3 Nc6(knight to c6 in response to Knight to f3), and bb5(bishop goes to b5). White usually fights for control of the center and Black has hard time getting an advantage in this opening.

Close

Caro Kann Defense

It is known for it's solidity and it's defining move is c6 in response to e4. It then goes to d5 without blocking the light-squared bishop. Which is considered the main drawback of it's cousin the French Defense.
